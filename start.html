Instruction for part 1

We are trying to develop computer programs that can understand common emotions, so they can communicate better with human users.

Rules of the game

Imagine you are a narrator. Today, you are narrating a story of John and Sally's wedding.

Your goal is to make your audience experience the emotion written on the left top in the yellow box.

As narrator, your job is to tell the story of John and Sally's wedding. We have provided you with the beginning of the story as well as two events that happen later in the story. Your job is to complete the story by filling in what happens in between these events. To do this, simply click the button labeled "Add connecting sentences", which will create a text box that you can use to continue the story.

As your story will be fed to a giant computer program, please follow the instructions below:

  • Do NOT refresh the page
  • Please write only one sentece per text box.
  • Please write the events in sequence of occurance
  • Please use a simple language. Do NOT use compound, complex, or conditional sentences.
  • Please use past tense and active voice.
  • Please use names, John and Sally, instead of pronouns where the need be.
  • On average it takes 3-5 sentences in each block, but you can write more if you want to
  • You can include character based events, e.g. 'A mother did something'
  • Your audiance should experience the given emotion

Instructions for part 2

In part 2, you have to make story sound more natural.

It is essential that you complete this part of the HIT, otherwise you will NOT get paid, there are no partial payments.

You need to write an "interesting" version for each sentence that you wrote earlier.

Example: Simple Sentence: "John prepared breakfast."

Interesting Sentence: "John prepared a delicious egg sandwich for breakfast."
